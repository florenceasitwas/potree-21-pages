    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Melchior Missirini,</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;Della chiesa priorale di S.     <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">S. Romeo:&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">probably a dialectical distortion (1).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">BUILDING HISTORY</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">The church of the holy Archbishop from Reims is probably from the time period of the French rule, so it was created in the late 8th or 9th century (2). It first served as lodgings for french pilgrims to Rome (3), but it was changed into a canonical monastery right before 1040 (4). In the Romanesque time, it apparently experienced changes and extensions (5).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">In the Gothic time, it was radically redesigned. The point in time can only be roughly estimated. It is possible that the extension of the forecourt, documented in 1303, was already connected to the conversion plan (6); the pier and vault seem to have been first constructed around 1350 (7). The windows of the side walls were expanded in the 15th century (8).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">In 1568 or 1581, the church was awarded the priority title (9). Subsequently, the Gothic style and the canon chancel blocking the center aisle was removed, and the choir stalls were moved to the middle choral chapel. At the same time, the staircase in front of the three choir chapels was redesigned and an organ loft with new side doors was installed (10). The final consecration took place in 1589 (11). The bell tower and the canonry were also built at that time (12). In the early 17th century, the inside room received a sequence of architectural altar tabernacles (13).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">In 1821, the outside walls were cleaned, and the portals were painted black and white according to the model of the medieval Florentine marble incrustations (14). At that time, the new Gothic window of the left choral chapel was created.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><strong><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">RECONSTRUCTION OF THE ORIGINAL BUILDINGS</span></strong></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">The Carolingian Pilgrim Hospital, 8th/9th Century&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Form unknown. &ndash; Perhaps the older of the two reconstructed churches in the following section is identical with the hospital church.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">The Romanesque Church; 11th/12th Century</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">remnants.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">A Romanesque side portal with a round arch and architrave has been preserved in the fa&ccedil;ade to the right of the main portal, together with a small wall segment; the round arch was partially removed later, so that a protective roof could be installed above it. A second Romanesque remnant is&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">the eastern third of the northern side wall.</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;It contains a narrow window, whose round arch is carved out of a stone in a strikingly ancient manner, in lieu of showing the wedge stone construction of the fa&ccedil;ade portal.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Conclusions.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">We also recognized possible remnants of the two different churches in both of these wall segments. The older one (with the window) could have originated in the early 11th century and the younger one around the 12th century &ndash; unless one prefers to place the older one in the Carolingian time period and the younger one in the 11th century. The first building must have been, in spite of the low position of the window, reconstructed as a church hall, since the wall segment reaches very far up. The second building was probably understood as an extension of the first and was, like the first, a rectangular, flat-roofed hall. It already has the dimensions of a Gothic building, because its corners coincide with the first&rsquo;s corners.</span><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&ndash; Next to the chair laid the canonry.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">The Gothic Buildings, First Half of the 14th Century</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Church.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Essentially preserved. The choir chapels had a narrow, high pointed arch window, as individual traces can be recognized. Similar windows were probably also added to the side walls. The canon chancel stood in the center aisle (15), probably a simple rectangular wall like the corresponding monks&apos; choirs in S. Maria Novella, S. Croce, S. Trinita, Ognissanti, etc., which were removed at the same time.</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Canonry</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">. The canons&apos; living quarters were grouped to the south of the church around a cloister, of which some remnants have been preserved. As a result, the south wall of the church was exposed; like the other sides, it was decorated with a round-arched frieze (remnants in the current, extended canonry).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><strong><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">BUILDING DESCRIPTION</span></strong></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Church</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><u><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Outside.&nbsp;</span></u></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Overview</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">. The church is rectangular in plan. The quarry stone walls of the fa&ccedil;ade and the north side contain the Romanesque remnants described in the &ldquo;Reconstruction&rdquo; section. Between these oldest parts, located at the south-west corner and the north-east corner of the church, are the uneven quarry stone walls of the Gothic reconstruction, which reveal themselves as a uniform structure due to the evenly running scaffolding holes; the rear wall and the bell tower, both with windows from the 16th and 19th centuries, also belong to this. Remnants of the plaster and paint from 1821 are on all the walls.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">The fa&ccedil;ade</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;has retained its Gothic nature to some extent. Its structure - a main portal and above it three wide-open round-arched windows, of which the central one is emphasized by its particular size and finer profiling, as well as a (renovated) round-arched frieze on the low gable - is inspired by the fa&ccedil;ade of S. Stefano. The only somewhat distinct motif,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">the main portal</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, has a pointed arch above a console-supported architrave; it is too generally stylized to allow a more exact positioning.</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">North wall</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">. Its windows appear to have been extended at a later date.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><u><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Inside.&nbsp;</span></u></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Illustrations.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Floor plan and interior views by Supino,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Gli albori</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;etc. 1906, 131 and panel XXVIII.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Framework.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Three-nave, three-bay pseudo-basilica with triangular choral chapels; pillars, pilasters, pointed arches and ribbed vaults everywhere. The walls are made of quarry stone, the limbs of house stone (Macigno).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Floor plan.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Center aisle bays are square and side aisle bays are longitudinal rectangular - as in S. Maria Maggiore. The main choir chapel is transversely rectangular. Side choirs are square, and all three choirs are closed off by a uniform straight rear wall - similar to Arnolfo&apos;s Badia.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Structure.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">A hall-like pseudo-basilica - like in S. Maria Maggiore. Similarly to S. Maria Maggiore, in the central nave, pilaster-like short wall attachments rise above the pillar capitals up to the vaults, which are set slightly higher than in the side aisles. The central nave pillars are octagonal - like in S. Croce; their capitals have coarsely embossed two-row acanthus leaves. The free pillars correspond to the walls and the pilasters on the chapel opening</span><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">- again like in S. Maria Maggiore; however, in this church, they are not polygonal and are instead flat and pilaster-like. The profile of the bases of the pillars and pilasters is also unique:</span><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">a slant above a slab, so it&rsquo;s a very concise, austere formulation. Between the side aisle vaults, the chords reach down strikingly low, similar to those in S. Maria Novella (16); the five-sided polygonal cross-section of the ribs was also prefigured in the Dominican church, whereas the five-sided pyramid shape of the consoles below was found in S. Croce. - The curved</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;staircase</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;in front of the choir chapels dates from 1589 -&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Overall effect:</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;coarse and unoriginal; the church appears to be the work of an eclectic who adopted motifs from all the more important Gothic buildings in the city and followed S. Maria Maggiore particularly closely.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Decoration.</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;The sparse architectural forms are enlivened with figures in a few places: heads on the capital of the second left-hand pillar and half-figures of St. Remigius on the keystone of the last nave bay. Originally, a&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">decorative fresco painting</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;would have made the room appear somewhat less sober.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">The altar tabernacles</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;with its Tuscan half-columns and its fragmented segmental pediments only reveal the frigid style of the 17th century through their ornamentation. They are said to have been made in Rome (cf. fn. 13) and are unified with the windows that were extended in the Quattrocento.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Canonry.&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">A Trecento cloister with octagonal pillars stretches along the south wall of the church. Segmental arches and square, ribless cross vaults. The first bay of its west wing has also been preserved. Above and next to it are the living quarters, which reveal themselves as the result of the extension of 1589 through many doors decorated with coats of arms.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><strong><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">DECOR</span></strong></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">CHURCH</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><u><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Outside.</span></u></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Facade.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Fresco in the tympanum of the portal, half-figure of St. Remigius, from Carlo Falcini, 1818 (17).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><u><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Inside.</span></u></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">First altar.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">(Patron: Bagnesi); built in 1629 (inscription). Relics in carved containers (18).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Second altar.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">(Patron: Fabbrini, named degli Aranci), built 1634 (inscription). Remnants of frescoes, saint with globe and others, 14th century (19).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Organ gallery;&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">dated 1583.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><u><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Choral chapels.&nbsp;</span></u></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Right side choir&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">(Patron: Beccuti) (20). Altarpiece and frescoes from 1821. Panel painting,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Madonna della Misericordia</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, by a contemporary of Cimabue, End of the 13th century (21).     <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">(7)&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Coat of arms.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">The coats of arms of the Bagnesi, Pepi and Alberti families are carved into the vault keystones, pillars and pilasters. Rosselli writes in his&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Sepoltuario</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;that Piero del Bene Pepi, who is named on the first southern pillar, was a member of the College of Priors in 1350.</span><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Richa I, 1754, 260 still saw the tombstone of this Piero; Cocchi,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Chiese</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, 1903, 136, 137). The date made probable by this would be consistent with the fact that the foliage of the coat of arms in the corresponding first pillar (Bagnesi) could hardly have been created before the middle of the Trecento; of course, this coat of arms is carved from a special stone and set into the pillar, but not chiseled into it. However, very similar stem and tendril forms can also be found on the keystone of the second bay in the left aisle. The quatrefoil frame on the coats of arms above the entrance to the right choral chapel also suggests a late date. The figurative architectural sculptures - small heads on the leaf ends of the second pillar capital on the left and the half-figure of Remigius in the keystone of the last nave bay in front of the choir - are too rough to allow a more precise dating.&nbsp;</span></p>

Remigio; Fantozzi,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Guida</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, 1842, 159, reversed the relationship between the two buildings, but moved S. Remigio down to 1428. Remigio to 1428, on which</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Marchese,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Memorie domenicane</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, 1854, 35, stated that the architecture of the church unequivocally spoke against such a late origin and spoke in favor of placing it in the time period of S. Maria Novella.&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Milanesi</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;(Vasari I, 655) balanced the &ldquo;pros&rdquo; and &ldquo;cons&rdquo; of this line of thought by saying that the connection of S. Remigio with S. Maria Novella and its architects was not proven in any case. In the meantime, the author of&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Notizie e guida</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">di Firenze</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, 1841, 309, 310 had put forward the equally contestable thesis that the church came from the school of Arnolfo di Cambio, followed by Jacob Burckhardt,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Cicerone</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;I, 1860, 142 and the Cicerone editors (Cicerone II, 1901, 10).&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Biadi</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, Fabbr., 1824, 48 dated the building to the 13th century. Supino</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, Gli Albori</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;etc., 1906, 130 in the 14th century. Cocchi,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Chiese</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, 1903, 136, 137 added the date 1350, which I analyzed.</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Paatz,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Werden und Wesen der Trecentoarchitektur in Toskana,</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;1937, 161 fn. 149. G. Marchini,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Rivista d&apos;Arte</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;XX, 1938, 108 mentions the church as an example of how Gothic architectural forms were Italianized in Florence.&nbsp;</span></p>

    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">S. REMIGIO</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Piazza S. Remigio</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">SPEZIALLITERATUR</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Melchior Missirini,&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Della chiesa priorale di S. Remigio, e del suo altare del SS. Sacramento; 1839. Zitiert als Missirini, S. Remigio, 1839.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">NAME</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">S. Romeo:&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">wohl mundartliche Enstellung (1).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">BAUGESCHICHTE&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Die Kirche des heiligen Erzbischofs von Reims ist wahrscheinlich zur Zeit der fr&auml;nkischen Herrschaft, also im sp&auml;ten 8. Oder 9. Jahrhundert, gegr&uuml;ndet worden (2). Sie diente zuerst als Herberge f&uuml;r fr&auml;nkische Rompilger (3), wurde aber bereits vor 1040 in ein Kanonikerstift verwandelt (4). Noch in romanischer Zeit erfuhr sie anscheinend Ver&auml;nderungen und Erweiterungen (5).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">In gotischer Zeit wurde sie eingreifend umgestaltet. Der Zeitpunkt l&auml;sst sich nur ungef&auml;hr angeben. M&ouml;glich, dass schon die urkundlich f&uuml;r 1303 beglaubigte Erweiterung des Vorplatzes mit dem Umbauplan in Zusammenhang stand (6); die Pfeiler und Gew&ouml;lbe scheinen jedoch erst gegen 1350 errichtet zu sein (7). Die Fenster der Seitenw&auml;nde wurden im 15. Jahrhundert erweitert.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">1568 oder 1581 wurde der Kirche der Prioreititel verliehen (9). Anschlie&szlig;end beseitigte man den gotischen, das Mittelschiff versperrenden Kanonikerchor und verlegte das Chorgest&uuml;hl in die mittlere Chorkapelle, indem man zugleich die Treppe vor den drei Chorkapellen umgestaltete und eine Orgelempore samt neuen Seitent&uuml;ren anbrachte (10). Die Schlussweihe fand 1589 statt (11). Auch der Glockenturm und das Kanonikat sind damals ausgebaut worden (12). Im fr&uuml;hen 17. Jahrhundert erhielt der Innenraum eine Folge von architektonischen Altartabernakeln (13).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">1821 wurden die Au&szlig;enmauern verputzt und die Portalleibungen nach dem Vorbild der mittelalterlichen Florentiner Marmorinkrustationen schwarzwei&szlig; bemalt (14). Damals entstand auch das neugotische Fenster der linken Chorkapelle.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">REKONSTRUKTION VERLORENER BAUZUST&Auml;NDE&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Das karolingische Pilgerhospital; 8./9. Jahrhundert.&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Gestalt unbekannt. &ndash;Vielleicht ist die &auml;ltere der beiden im folgenden Abschnitt rekonstruierten Kirchen mit der Spital-Kirche identisch.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Die romanische Kirche; 11./12. Jahrhundert.&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Reste.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">In der Fassade hat sich rechts neben dem Hauptportal ein romanisches&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Seitenportal&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">mit Rundbogen und Architrav erhalten, zusammen mit einem kleinen Mauerst&uuml;ck; der Rundbogen ist sp&auml;ter zum Teil abgearbeitet worden, um ein Schutzdach dar&uuml;ber anbringen zu k&ouml;nnen. Ein zweiter romanischer Rest ist&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">das &ouml;stliche Drittel der n&ouml;rdlichen Seitenmauer.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Es enth&auml;lt ein schmales Fenster, dessen Rundbogen in auffallend altert&uuml;mlicher Weise aus einem Stein herausgemei&szlig;elt ist, anstatt die Keilsteinkonstruktion des Fassadenportals zu zeigen.</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Schlussfolgerungen.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Wir haben also in diesen beiden Mauerst&uuml;cken vielleicht Reste zweier verschiedener Kirchen zu erkennen. Der &auml;ltere (mit dem Fenster) k&ouml;nnte im fr&uuml;hen 11. Jahrhundert entstanden sein, der j&uuml;ngere etwa im 12. Jahrhundert &ndash; falls man es nicht vorzieht, den &auml;lteren in karolingische Zeit und den j&uuml;ngeren ins 11. Jahrhundert zu setzen. Der erste Bau muss trotz der niedrigen Lage des Fensters als eine Saalkirche rekonstruiert werden, da das Mauerst&uuml;ck sehr weit hinaufreicht. Der zweite Bau ist wohl als eine Erweiterung des ersten zu verstehen und wird wie dieser ein rechteckiger, flachgedeckter Saal gewesen sein. Er hatte bereits die Abmessungen des gotischen Baus, denn seine Ecken fallen mit dessen Ecken zusammen. &ndash; Neben der Kirche lag das Kanonikat.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Die gotischen Bauten; 1. H. 14. Jahrh.&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Kirche.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Im Wesentlichen erhalten. Die Chorkapellen hatten je ein schmales, hohes Spitzbogen-Fenster, wie einzelne Spuren erkennen lassen. &Auml;hnliche Fenster sind wohl auch in den Seitenw&auml;nden zu erg&auml;nzen. Im Mittelschiff stand der Kanonikerchor (15), wahrscheinlich ein einfaches Mauerrechteck wie die entsprechenden, gleichzeitig beseitigten M&ouml;nchsch&ouml;re in S. Maria Novella, S. Croce, S. Trinita, Ognissanti usw.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Kanonikat.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Die Wohnr&auml;ume der Kanoniker gruppierten sich s&uuml;dlich von der Kirche um einen Kreuzgang, von dem sich einige Reste erhalten haben. Die S&uuml;dwand der Kirche lag infolgedessen frei; sie war wie die anderen Seiten mit einem Rundbogenfries geschm&uuml;ckt (Reste im jetzigen, erweiterten Kanonikat).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-align:center; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">BAUBESCHREIBUNG</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Kirche</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><u><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Au&szlig;en&nbsp;</span></u></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&Uuml;bersicht.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Die Kirche ist im Grundri&szlig; rechteckig. Die Bruchsteinmauern der Fassade und der Nordseite enthalten die romanischen Reste, die im Abschnitt &ldquo;Rekonstruktion&rdquo; beschrieben worden sind. Zwischen diesen &auml;ltesten, bei der S&uuml;dwestecke und der Nordostecke der Kirche gelegenen Teilen erstreckt sich das ungleichm&auml;&szlig;ige Bruchstein-Gem&auml;uer des gotischen Umbaus, das sich durch die gleichm&auml;&szlig;ig durchlaufenden Ger&uuml;stl&ouml;cher als eine einheitliche Anlage verr&auml;t; zugeh&ouml;rig auch die R&uuml;ckwand und der Glockenturm, beide mit Fenstern aus dem 16. und 19. Jahrhundert. An allen W&auml;nden Reste des Putzes und der Bemalung von 1821.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Die Fassade&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">hat ihren gotischen Charakter einigerma&szlig;en bewahrt. Ihre Gliederung &ndash; ein Hauptportal und dar&uuml;ber drei weitge&ouml;ffnete Rundbogenfenster, von denen das mittlere durch besondere Gr&ouml;&szlig;e und feinere Profilierung hervorgehoben ist, sowie ein (erneuerter) RUndbogenfries am niedrigen Giebel &ndash; ist von der Fassade von S. Stefano angeregt. Das einzige etwas ausgepr&auml;gte Motiv,&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">das Hauptportal</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">, hat einen Spitzbogen &uuml;ber einem konsolengetragenen Architrav; es ist zu allgemein stilisiert, um eine genauere Ansetzung zu erlauben.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Nordwand.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Ihre Fenster scheinen nachtr&auml;glich erweitert zu sein.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><u><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Innen</span></u></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Abbildungen.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Grundriss und Innenansichten bei Supino, Gli albori etc. 1906, 131 und Tafel XXVIII.</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">System.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Dreischiffige dreijochige Pseudobasilika mit dreieckigen Chorkapellen; allenthalben Pfeiler, Wandpfeiler, Spitzb&ouml;gen und Kreuzrippengew&ouml;lbe. Die Mauern bestehen aus Bruchstein, die Glieder aus Haustein (Macigno).&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Grundriss.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Mittelschiffsjoche quadratisch, Seitenschiffsjoche l&auml;ngsrechteckig &ndash; wie in S. Maria Maggiore. Hauptchorkapelle quer rechteckig. Nebench&ouml;re quadratisch, alle drei Ch&ouml;re von einer einheitlichen geraden R&uuml;ckwand abgeschlossen &ndash; &auml;hnlich wie in Arnolfos Badia.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Aufbau.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Hallenartig psuedobasilikal &ndash; wie in S. Maria Maggiore. Wie dort steigen im Mittelschiff &uuml;ber den Pfeilerkapitellen pilasterartige kurze Wandvorlagen bis zu den Gew&ouml;lben auf, die etwas h&ouml;her ansetzen als in den Seitenschiffen. Die Mittelschiffspfeiler sind achteckig &ndash; wie in S. Croce; ihre Kapitelle haben derb bossiertes zweireihiges Akanthusblatt. Den Frepfeilern entsprechen an den W&auml;nden und an den Kapellen&ouml;ffnungen Wandpfeiler &ndash; wiederum wie in S. Maria Maggiore; sie sind hier aber nicht polygonal, sondern pilasterartig flach. Individuell wirkt auch das Profil der Basen der Pfeiler und Wandpfeiler: eine Schr&auml;ge &uuml;ber einer Platte, also eine sehr knappe, herbe Formulierung. Zwischen den Seitenschiffsgew&ouml;lben reichen die Gurte auffallend tief herab, &auml;hnlich wie in S. Maria Novella (16); auch der f&uuml;nfseitig-polygonale Querschnitt der Rippen war in der Dominikanerkirche vorgebildet, die f&uuml;nfseitige Pyramidengestalt der darunter befindlichen Konsolen dagegen in S. Croce. &ndash; Die geschwungene&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Treppe&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">vor den Chorkapellen stammt von 1589. &ndash;&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Gesamtwirkung:&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">derb und unoriginell; die Kirche scheint das Werk eines Eklektikers zu sein, der von allen bedeutenderen gotischen Bauten der Stadt Motive &uuml;bernahm und sich besonders eng an S. Maria Maggiore anschloss.&nbsp;</span></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></em></p>
    <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:115%; font-size:12pt;"><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Dekoration.&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Die sp&auml;rlichen architektonischen Formen sind an einigen wenigen Stellen fig&uuml;rlich belebt: am Kapitell des zweiten linken Pfeilers K&ouml;pfe, am Schlu&szlig;stein des letzten Mittelschiffsjochs Halbfigure des heiligen Remigius. Urspr&uuml;nglich wird eine&nbsp;</span><em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">Freskobemalung&nbsp;</span></em><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';">dekorativer Art den Raum etwas weniger n&uuml;chtern haben erscheinen lassen.&nbsp;</span></p>
